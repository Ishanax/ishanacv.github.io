My coding journey

I had always had something against a job behind a computer, believing that I wanted to do something more active and social. After finishing my Master in Philosophy I got a job as a deckhand on a yacht and sailed across the Atlantic ocean. This made me completely reconsider what I wanted in life and I was instantly ready to settle down. When the coronavirus crisis started I was forced indoors, so I thought it might be fun to try out coding. I quickly found out I really enjoyed it, it’s one of the rare activities that can make me forget about time and completely absorb me. I started out practising small coding ‘challenges’ in Python, before moving on to an online course, where I also learned some SQLite. When I explored all the different types of software development jobs I found I lean most toward front end web development. From then on I focussed on learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap and responsive web design. While this gave me a good overview of software development, I’m certainly still eager to learn more. Currently, I’m focussing on developing my first application using React. In the end, I would say learning to code on my own was difficult and lonely at times, but it has taught me a great deal of independence and self-discipline.

Philosophy skills

As a philosophy student, I enjoyed practising logic which I have found very useful during coding too. Furthermore, the critical and analytical way of thinking that was necessary for philosophy helps me tackle coding problems. Philosophy equipped me with the ability to learn in new and unfamiliar areas and think deeply about abstract problems. Studying philosophy I had to change the way I think, which I also find myself having to when coding.
